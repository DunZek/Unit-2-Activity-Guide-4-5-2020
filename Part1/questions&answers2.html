12. Read Pages 139 to 140. Describe the program of collectivization under Stalin. How did this program conflict with the nationalist feelings or interests of the groups most affected by the program?

Collectivization is the act of congregating the forfeited properties of Individuals for the state or larger nation to use. During Stalin's rule, he collectivized farms to ensure the Soviet Union could maintain a stowage of food and ensure efficient crop production. This program threatened the personal interests of people in order to uphold the bigger nation's common interests. Thus, it hurt the nationalist feelings of those people.
