13. What was the situation (political, economic, social) in Germany following the signing of the Treaty of Versailles in 1919?

Basically, Germany was in a pickle:

  • Political:
    • Political parties were unsuccessful at running a successful government. It took the German people desperateness for the Nazi party to be elected into power.
  • Economical:
    • Germany's economy had already been wrecked from the consequences of the Great war: Germany owed US debt in order to help rebuild the country, the Great Depression ruined everyone, Germany lost its colonies and it had to pay war reparations because of the Treaty of Versailles. To top it all off, Germany thought that they could pay off all of its financial problems by hyperinflating.
  • Social:
    • There was tension between people and animosity had grown towards the Jews as they had become the scapegoat for all of Germany's problems. Those that had been deemed undesirable were treated with contempt. German society started to become more and more ultranationalistic over the years.

14. How did national pride and self-interest help with Hitler’s rise to power in 1932-33?

Hitler had instilled a strong sense of national pride in his people, so they started to believe aligned with Germany as a whole. Hitler's charisma had helped him make himself look as the best leader they could ever have during Germany's darkest days.

15. How did Hitler use militarism and propaganda to rally the German people?

Hitler's propaganda rallied the Germn people by instilling nationalism in them. He made them believe that the greatest cause is the German cause, that he was Germany's greatest leader and that they needed to get rid of "un-german" things in society and their enemies such as Jews, anti-German and anti-Nazi individuals. Militarism in German society gave hope to the German people, that all is not lost and gave them the confidence in Germany and in Hitler's leadership.


Additionally, Hitler's propaganda aroused fear and anger onto opponents, justified defeat and victories, emotionally appealed to people using symbols and persuaded individuals for the cause.

16. Why did many people hope that appeasement was the best response to German expansion?

People hoped that it would stop German expansion because they believed that appeasement would help avoid conflict and create peace instead. After the horrible consequences of the Great War, people didn't want to stop Germany using force again.

17. Why did the leaders of Britain, France and Italy allow Germany’s takeover of the Sudetenland to go unchallenged?

They believed that Hitler would stop the expansion of German territory if he were given the Sudetenland.


British prime minister Neville Chamberlain: "the peoples of the British empire were at one with those of Germany, of France, and of Italy... in their anxiety, their intense desire for peace."

18. What event in 1939 triggered Britain and France to declare war on Germany?

The event in 1939 that triggered Britain and France to declare war on Germany was Germany's invasion of Poland.

19. How did Canada’s entry into WWII differ from its entry into WWI?

In contrast to WWI, Canada entered WWII on it's own will, free from British dependancy and ties.

20. What event in WWII led the Canadian and American governments to round up and inter thousands of Japanese Canadians?

The attack of Pearl Harbour and Hong Kongby Japanese forces made the Canadian and American governments resent their own Japanese citizens.

21. What organization was formed after WWII to help keep the peace? Where are its headquarters located? Who is the current Secretary-General?

The United Natons (UN) was formed as a response to the destruction caused by WWII. It is headquartered currently in New York, USA, and the current Secretary-General of the UN is Antonio Guterres.
